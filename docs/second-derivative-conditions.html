3.8 Second derivative conditions

Just like in one dimension, we usually look at the second derivative of a function to classify a point as being a local maximum or a local minimum. The situation for higher dimensions is similar, where instead we look at the quadratic form consisting of the even degree terms of the Taylor polynomial.

Theorem 3.17 (Signature of a critical point) Let \(U\subset \mathbb{R}^n\) be an open set, and let \(f:U\to\mathbb{R}\) be of class ^2. Let \(\boldsymbol{a}\in U\) be a critical point of \(f\). The signature of the critical point \(\boldsymbol{a}\) is the signature of the quadratic form
